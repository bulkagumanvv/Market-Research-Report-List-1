<p><p>The multivalent vaccines market includes several types: Conjugate vaccines link antigens to carriers, enhancing immune response against bacteria. Inactivated and subunit vaccines contain killed pathogens or specific proteins, respectively, ensuring safety while provoking immunity. Live attenuated vaccines use weakened viruses or bacteria for robust immune activation. Toxoid vaccines target toxins produced by pathogens to prevent diseases like tetanus. Recombinant vaccines utilize genetic engineering to produce antigens, allowing targeted immune responses, reflecting advancements in vaccine technology.</p></p>
