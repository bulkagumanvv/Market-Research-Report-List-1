<p><ul><li>Slow Freezing</li><li>Rapid Freezing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The egg freezing service market primarily consists of two types: slow freezing and rapid freezing. Slow freezing, also known as conventional cryopreservation, involves gradually lowering the temperature of eggs, allowing for potential cellular ice crystal formation which can sometimes compromise cell integrity. Rapid freezing, or vitrification, employs ultra-fast cooling methods that prevent ice crystal formation, ensuring higher survival rates for eggs post-thaw. Both methods cater to women seeking to preserve fertility for future family planning, each offering distinct advantages and success rates.</p></p>
